India

A country in South Asia — brief summary below

India, officially the Republic of India, is a country in South Asia. It is the world's most populous democracy and the second-most populous country after China. India is known for its diverse cultures, languages, religions, landscapes, and long history.

Quick facts: Capital — New Delhi; Largest city — Mumbai; Official languages — Hindi and English (associate official); Currency — Indian Rupee (INR); Area — ~3.287 million km²; Population — ~1.4 billion (2025 est.).

History

Events and eras that shaped the nation

India's history stretches back millennia. The Indus Valley Civilization (c. 2600–1900 BCE) was one of the world's earliest urban cultures. Over centuries, the subcontinent saw the rise and fall of many empires and kingdoms, including the Maurya and Gupta Empires, the Delhi Sultanate, and the Mughal Empire. From the 18th century, large parts of India came under British colonial rule, culminating in the British Raj. India gained independence in 1947 and became a democratic republic in 1950.

Major periods

Key historical eras listed
  • Ancient (Indus Valley, Vedic period, Mahajanapadas)
  • Classical (Maurya, Gupta)
  • Medieval (Regional kingdoms, Delhi Sultanate, Vijayanagara, Mughal)
  • Colonial (European trade, British East India Company, British Raj)
  • Modern (Independence movement, partition 1947, post-independence development)

geography

Physical features, borders and regions

India occupies most of the Indian subcontinent. It shares land borders with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar, and maritime borders with Sri Lanka and the Maldives. The geography is varied: the Himalayan mountain range in the north, fertile plains of the Ganges and Indus, the Thar Desert in the west, and tropical regions in the south.

climate

Weather patterns and the monsoon

India has multiple climate zones — from alpine in the Himalayas to tropical in the south. The monsoon (June–September) is a defining seasonal phenomenon, bringing most of the country's annual rainfall and shaping agriculture and livelihoods.

demographics

Population, languages and religions

India is extremely diverse ethnically, linguistically, and religiously. Major religions include Hinduism (majority), Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, Jainism, and many tribal beliefs. India has hundreds of languages; the Constitution recognizes several scheduled languages, and Hindi and English are used for official purposes.

Population trends

Growth, age structure and urbanisation
  • Rapid population growth in the 20th century, slowing in the 21st.
  • Large youth population; median age rising slowly as fertility declines.
  • High urbanization with continuing significant rural population.

Culture

Traditions, arts, cuisine and festivals

Indian culture is a mosaic of traditions, languages, religions, cuisines, arts, music, dance forms, and festivals. Classical music (Hindustani and Carnatic), various dance styles (Bharatanatyam, Kathak, Odissi), and film industries (especially Bollywood) are globally influential.

Festivals

Major religious and regional celebrations
  • Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, Navaratri, Pongal, Onam, Gurpurab, and many regional festivals.

Economy

Economic sectors and growth drivers

India has a mixed economy with agriculture, manufacturing, and services. In recent decades, the services sector (IT, finance, business process outsourcing) has driven much of the growth. India is one of the world's largest economies by nominal GDP and PPP. Key challenges include income inequality, infrastructure needs, and job creation for a large workforce.

Key sectors

Industries important to the economy
  • Information technology and software services
  • Pharmaceuticals and biotechnology
  • Agriculture and agribusiness
  • Manufacturing (automotive, textiles, steel)
  • Energy and renewables

Government and politics

How the country is governed

India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic. The President is the head of state and the Prime Minister is the head of government. India has a multi-party system, with the Parliament consisting of two houses: the Lok Sabha (House of the People) and the Rajya Sabha (Council of States).

Constitution

Founding legal document and principles

The Constitution of India (1950) establishes the framework for political principles, fundamental rights, directive principles, and federal structure. It is one of the longest constitutions in the world.

Tourism

Places and travel highlights

India attracts tourists to its historical monuments, cultural experiences, wildlife, hill stations, beaches, and spiritual sites. Popular destinations include the Taj Mahal (Agra), Jaipur and other cities of Rajasthan, Kerala backwaters, Goa beaches, Ladakh, and Varanasi.

Travel tips

Practical advice for visitors
  • Plan around the monsoon if traveling to regions affected by heavy rains.
  • Use registered guides for heritage sites and national parks.
  • Respect local customs and dress codes at religious places.

Science, technology & education

Research, space, IT and learning

India has a strong presence in science and technology, notable for its space program (ISRO), information technology exports, and an expanding research ecosystem in universities and institutes. Education is a priority, though access and quality vary across regions.
